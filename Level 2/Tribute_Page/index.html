K. Kamaraj

Kumaraswami Kamaraj (15 July 1903 – 2 October 1975), popularly known as Kamarajar

Here's a time line of K. Kamaraj's life:

    Early Life and Entry into Politics

  • 1903 - Born on July 15 in Virudhunagar, Tamil Nadu.
  • 1919 - Joined the Indian National Congress at the age of 16, inspired by the freedom movement.
  • 1920-1927 - Actively participated in the Non-Cooperation Movement and later the Civil Disobedience Movement.

    • Political Ascendancy and Role in Freedom Struggle

  • 1930 - Imprisoned for participating in the Salt Satyagraha led by Mahatma Gandhi.
  • 1942 - Played a significant role in the Quit India Movement, again leading to imprisonment.

    • Post-Independence Leadership

  • 1954 - Became the Chief Minister of Madras (now Tamil Nadu).
  • 1957 - Re-elected as Chief Minister, initiating several educational and infrastructure projects.
  • 1962 - Elected for the third time as Chief Minister, emphasizing rural development and industrialization.

    • Contributions to Education

  • 1954-1963 - Implemented the Midday Meal Scheme to reduce dropout rates and encourage education among children.
  • 1957 - Introduced free education for children up to the 11th standard and built numerous schools, increasing literacy rates in Tamil Nadu.

    • Political Strategy and Influence

  • 1963 - Resigned as Chief Minister to focus on national politics and was instrumental in the rise of Lal Bahadur Shastri as Prime Minister after Nehru’s death.
  • 1963-1969 - Served as the President of the Indian National Congress, playing a crucial role in party reorganization and political strategy.

    • Later Years and Legacy

  • 1969 - Faced internal party conflicts and was part of the faction that formed the Indian National Congress (Organization) after the party split.
  • 1971 - Defeated in the Lok Sabha elections but remained an influential political figure until his death.
  • 1975 - Awarded the Bharat Ratna, India’s highest civilian award, for his service to the nation.
  • 1975 - Passed away on October 2, leaving behind a legacy of dedication, simplicity, and public service.

• Education: Pioneered the midday meal scheme and free education in Tamil Nadu.
• Infrastructure: Focused on rural development, electrification, and industrial growth.
• Political Leadership: Played a pivotal role in the Congress party and Indian politics post-independence.
